# Domain Prompts for Novel Discovery

This document contains example sub-domain prompts for each domain defined in the codebase, targeting novel discovery.

## Artificial Intelligence
**Description:** AI/ML models, neural networks, and intelligent systems
**Prompt:** Propose a novel neural architecture that dynamically adjusts its own topology during training to optimize for both energy efficiency and task performance, effectively mimicking neuroplasticity in biological brains.

## Astrobiology
**Description:** Life's origin, evolution, and distribution in the universe, including prebiotic chemistry and exoplanet biosignatures
**Prompt:** Develop a theoretical model for detecting "shadow biospheres" on Earth—microbial life with radically different biochemistry (e.g., opposite chirality or alternative nucleotides)—that may have co-evolved with known life but remains undetected.

## Astrophysics
**Description:** Celestial mechanics, stellar dynamics, and cosmic phenomena
**Prompt:** Formulate a hypothesis for the origin of Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) that unifies magnetar activity with potential interactions involving primordial black holes, and propose an observational campaign to test this.

## Autonomous Systems
**Description:** Robotics, autonomous vehicles, and self-governing systems
**Prompt:** Design a decentralized control algorithm for a swarm of autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) that allows them to self-organize into complex structures for deep-sea exploration without any central communication hub.

## Biology
**Description:** Biological systems and processes
**Prompt:** Investigate the potential for a "universal immune system" mechanism in multicellular organisms that could theoretically be activated to defend against unknown pathogens by recognizing fundamental entropic patterns of infection rather than specific antigens.

## Blockchain Systems
**Description:** Cryptoeconomics, decentralized consensus, tokenomics, and blockchain protocol design
**Prompt:** Propose a novel consensus mechanism that utilizes "Proof of Useful Work" where the computational power expended solves real-world scientific problems (e.g., protein folding) while maintaining the security and decentralization of the network.

## Chemical Engineering
**Description:** Process design, reactor engineering, and chemical systems
**Prompt:** Design a scalable, continuous-flow reactor system capable of synthesizing complex pharmaceutical compounds using only water and air as feedstocks, powered entirely by ambient thermal energy gradients.

## Chemistry
**Description:** Chemical reactions, kinetics, and thermodynamics
**Prompt:** Theorize a stable, room-temperature superconductor based on a novel arrangement of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) doped with rare-earth elements, and predict its critical temperature using density functional theory.

## Climate Geoengineering
**Description:** Deliberate large-scale climate interventions, including solar radiation management and carbon removal strategies
**Prompt:** Develop a comprehensive model for a stratospheric aerosol injection system that uses self-levitating nanobots to dynamically adjust albedo in real-time, minimizing regional weather disruption while maximizing cooling.

## Climate Science
**Description:** Climate modeling, carbon dynamics, and environmental systems
**Prompt:** Construct a predictive model that identifies potential "tipping points" in the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) triggered by specific, localized freshwater influx events from Greenland, with a focus on early warning signals.

## Cognitive Science
**Description:** Computational models of human cognition, decision-making, memory, and NeuroAI approaches
**Prompt:** Propose a computational model of "insight" or "aha!" moments that explains how the brain restructures problem representations, and demonstrate how this could be implemented in an AI system to solve riddles.

## Coding
**Description:** Software engineering, algorithms, and toolchain automation
**Prompt:** Design a self-healing software architecture that uses a genetic algorithm to automatically patch vulnerabilities and optimize code execution paths in real-time without human intervention or service interruption.

## Complex Systems
**Description:** Emergent phenomena, self-organization, chaos theory, and multi-agent systems across domains
**Prompt:** Develop a mathematical framework to predict the onset of synchronization in chaotic networks of coupled oscillators with time-varying delays, applying it to model the stability of power grids with high renewable penetration.

## Computational Finance
**Description:** Financial modeling, risk analysis, and algorithmic trading
**Prompt:** Create a market model that integrates behavioral economics principles with quantum game theory to predict flash crashes driven by high-frequency algorithmic trading feedback loops.

## Cybersecurity
**Description:** Security protocols, cryptography, and threat modeling
**Prompt:** Design a "polymorphic" encryption protocol that continuously changes its encryption method and key structure based on the detected entropy of the network traffic, making it mathematically resistant to quantum decryption attacks.

## Data Science
**Description:** Big data, statistical modeling, and predictive analytics
**Prompt:** Develop a novel dimensionality reduction technique that preserves both local and global topological structures of high-dimensional datasets better than t-SNE or UMAP, specifically optimized for analyzing single-cell genomic data.

## Economics
**Description:** Economic models, markets, and policy simulations
**Prompt:** Formulate a post-scarcity economic model that quantifies "reputation" and "contribution" as primary currencies, and simulate its stability in a closed-loop community with automated production.

## Energy Systems
**Description:** Power generation, distribution, and energy optimization
**Prompt:** Design a grid-scale energy storage system utilizing "gravity batteries" in abandoned mine shafts, optimizing the lift/drop cycles with AI to smooth out fluctuations from wind and solar sources.

## Engineering
**Description:** Engineering systems, design, and optimization
**Prompt:** Conceptualize a self-assembling bridge structure composed of modular, drone-like units that can dynamically reconfigure to support varying loads and span different gaps in disaster relief scenarios.

## Fluid Dynamics
**Description:** Fluid flow, turbulence, and hydrodynamic systems
**Prompt:** Derive a new turbulence closure model that accurately predicts drag reduction in non-Newtonian fluid flows through micro-channels, with applications in lab-on-a-chip devices.

## Fluid Mechanics
**Description:** Fluid behavior, flow analysis, and mechanical systems
**Prompt:** Investigate the hydrodynamics of a "super-cavitating" underwater vehicle that uses a generated gas bubble to reduce skin friction to near zero, proposing a hull shape that maintains stability at supersonic underwater speeds.

## General
**Description:** General mathematical or cross-domain problems
**Prompt:** Explore the isomorphism between the spread of misinformation in social networks and the propagation of viruses in biological networks, identifying a "vaccination" strategy for information ecosystems.

## Geosciences
**Description:** Earth sciences, geology, and geophysical processes
**Prompt:** Develop a model to predict volcanic eruptions by analyzing the subtle frequency shifts in seismic noise caused by magma chamber inflation, potentially increasing warning times from days to weeks.

## Materials Science
**Description:** Advanced materials, nanotechnology, and material properties
**Prompt:** Design a metamaterial with a negative refractive index in the visible spectrum that is flexible and breathable, enabling the creation of a practical "invisibility cloak" for thermal and visual camouflage.

## Mathematics
**Description:** Pure and applied mathematical reasoning and analysis
**Prompt:** Attempt to prove or disprove the existence of a "perfect cuboid" (a Euler brick where the space diagonal is also an integer), or develop a new class of Diophantine equations that could shed light on its existence.

## Medicine
**Description:** Medical and healthcare problems
**Prompt:** Propose a novel gene therapy delivery vector based on modified exosomes that can cross the blood-brain barrier and specifically target glioblastoma cells without triggering an immune response.

## Metrology
**Description:** Measurement science, calibration, and standards
**Prompt:** Design a quantum sensor based on entangled atomic clocks capable of detecting gravitational waves in the millihertz frequency range, effectively creating a table-top gravitational wave observatory.

## Neuroscience
**Description:** Neural systems, brain function, and cognitive modeling
**Prompt:** Hypothesize a mechanism for how long-term memories are encoded in the specific methylation patterns of DNA within neurons, and propose an experiment to read these "molecular engrams."

## Network Science
**Description:** Complex networks, graph theory, and connectivity analysis
**Prompt:** Develop an algorithm to identify "influential spreaders" in a temporal network where connections change over time, and apply it to optimize the dissemination of critical information during a crisis.

## Physics
**Description:** Physical systems, mechanics, and fundamental interactions
**Prompt:** Propose a theoretical experiment to test the "simulation hypothesis" by looking for pixelation artifacts or processing lag in the fundamental constants of the universe at the Planck scale.

## Public Health
**Description:** Population health, epidemiology, and policy modeling
**Prompt:** Design a predictive model for zoonotic spillover events that integrates satellite data on deforestation, wildlife migration patterns, and local human economic activity to identify high-risk hotspots.

## Quantum Biology
**Description:** Quantum effects in living systems, including photosynthesis efficiency, enzyme tunneling, and magnetoreception
**Prompt:** Investigate the role of quantum coherence in the avian magnetic compass, proposing a mechanism by which radical pair reactions in cryptochrome proteins are protected from thermal noise.

## Quantum Computing
**Description:** Quantum algorithms, hardware, and quantum information
**Prompt:** Develop a quantum algorithm for simulating high-temperature superconductivity in Hubbard models that offers an exponential speedup over classical Monte Carlo methods.

## Renewable Energy
**Description:** Solar, wind, and sustainable energy systems
**Prompt:** Design a bio-hybrid solar cell that integrates photosynthetic proteins with graphene layers to achieve a quantum efficiency exceeding the Shockley-Queisser limit.

## Robotics
**Description:** Robot control, motion planning, and embodied intelligence
**Prompt:** Create a control system for a soft robot that mimics the movement of an octopus, utilizing distributed processing in the limbs to handle complex manipulation tasks without central brain involvement.

## Signal Processing
**Description:** Signal analysis, filtering, and communication systems
**Prompt:** Develop a "cognitive radio" algorithm that uses deep reinforcement learning to dynamically exploit underutilized spectrum bands in real-time, maximizing throughput in a crowded RF environment.

## Social Science
**Description:** Human behavior, societal dynamics, and policy analysis
**Prompt:** Model the societal impact of Universal Basic Income (UBI) using an agent-based simulation that accounts for psychological factors like "meaning" and "status," predicting long-term effects on innovation and social cohesion.

## Space Technology
**Description:** Space exploration, satellite systems, and aerospace engineering
**Prompt:** Design a propulsion system for interstellar travel based on a "magnetic sail" that harvests momentum from the interstellar medium, calculating the travel time to Proxima Centauri.

## Synthetic Biology
**Description:** Engineered biological systems and synthetic organisms
**Prompt:** Engineer a synthetic bacterium capable of degrading plastic waste in the ocean and converting it into a harmless, biodegradable biomass, with a built-in "kill switch" to prevent uncontrolled proliferation.

## Systems Biology
**Description:** Biological networks, metabolic pathways, and cellular systems
**Prompt:** Construct a whole-cell computational model of a minimal cell that integrates gene expression, metabolism, and cell division, predicting the phenotypic consequences of every possible single-gene knockout.

## Unconventional Computing
**Description:** Biological and molecular computing paradigms, including DNA computing and cellular computing
**Prompt:** Design a logic gate architecture based on reaction-diffusion systems in chemical gels that can perform universal computation and self-repair after physical damage.
